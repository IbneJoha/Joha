🌐 About Me

Md. Ibne Joha is a graduate researcher at the Wireless Communications and Artificial Intelligence (WiComAI) Lab, Kookmin University, Seoul, South Korea, pursuing his M.Sc. in Electronics Engineering with a perfect CGPA of 4.5/4.5. His research focuses on AI-driven solutions for Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), edge computing, and smart energy management, including work on federated learning techniques. His work integrates deep learning and machine learning models with real-time energy forecasting, anomaly detection, NILM, and object tracking within secure, resource-constrained environments.

He has contributed to multiple government and industry-funded projects involving real-time photovoltaic (PV) power forecasting across 10 solar sites. Joha has made significant contributions through multiple publications in reputed journals, including Sensors, ICT Express, Applied Sciences, IEEE Access, and the World Electric Vehicle Journal, among others. His proposed NILM model has demonstrated significant improvements in disaggregation accuracy on both the REDD dataset and real-world energy consumption data.

His achievements include the International Excellent Researcher Scholarship, the Excellent Researcher Award, and the Best Paper Award at IEEE ICPC2T 2024. He is proficient in TensorFlow, PyTorch, Python, C/C++ and hardware platforms including Raspberry Pi, Jetson Nano, LattePanda, FPGA, ESP32, and LoRa.
