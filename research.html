Md. Ibne Joha’s research lies at the intersection of AI, edge computing, and intelligent autonomous systems, addressing critical challenges in real-time performance, scalability, and reliability for next-generation IoT and robotic applications. His current work focuses on autonomous navigation for both UAVs and UGVs, as well as IoT- and AI-enabled honeybee colony health monitoring, integrating advanced sensing, perception, and decision-making frameworks within resource-constrained environments. By leveraging deep learning, machine learning, and Edge AI techniques, he develops efficient, deployable models that bridge the gap between theoretical innovation and real-world implementation. His research emphasizes robust and adaptive systems for dynamic environments, contributing to advancements in robotics, precision agriculture, smart energy management, andintelligent monitoring systems. Through interdisciplinary approaches, Joha continues to advance the frontiers of AIoT, autonomous systems, and embedded intelligence for sustainable and impactful real-world applications..

⚡ Energy & AI

⚡ Energy Funded Project
One-Day Ahead Solar PV Power Generation Forecasting
Advanced AI models deployed across 10 PV sites to forecast solar power generation. Uses temperature, wind speed, humidity, cloud cover, and power aggregation features. Achieves RMSE < 8%. Government and company-funded project.

🏭 IIoT & AI

🏭 IIoT Published
AI-Driven Real-Time Load Forecasting & Anomaly Detection in Secure IIoT
Secure IIoT framework using a TCN-GRU-Attention model for real-time energy optimization. Isolation Forest achieves 95% Precision, 98% Recall, 96% F1. Secured with TLS/SSL and encrypted credentials. Deployable on edge SBCs.

📲 Edge AI & NILM

📲 Edge AI and NILM Under Review
ARBGTU-Net-NILM: Efficient AI for Real-time NILM in Secure Edge Computing
Proposed model enhances NILM performance with 51.27% F1-score improvement, reduces MAE by 38.28% and SAE by 89.83% vs. state-of-the-art. Designed for edge deployment with TLS/SSL security. Validated on REDD and real-world datasets.

🌍 IoT & AI

🌍 IoT Published
AI Models for Real-Time IoT-based Environmental Monitoring & Power Forecasting
IoT platform for real-time data collection. Hybrid LSTM-GRU model achieves 2.2% MAE, 4.91% RMSE, and 96.06% R² for air-conditioning power forecasting, outperforming individual models.

🚁 UAV & Control

🚁 UAV Funded
Real-time Object Tracking with Adaptive Zoom & Fuzzy Logic Gimbal Control
Government-funded project combining an AI-driven system that integrates real-time ROI extraction, deep learning-based object tracking, and fuzzy logic-based gimbal control to maintain stable OCC performance at target speeds up to 10 km/h. By combining deep learning with 5th-degree polynomial curve fitting, it adaptively adjusts zoom and focus based on object motion, ensuring precise alignment and optimal image sharpness in dynamic environments.
🚁 UAV Under Review
Optimizing Energy Efficiency in Hybrid UAVs Using DQN-Based Energy Management
Applies Deep Q-Network (DQN) reinforcement learning to optimize energy management in hybrid UAVs, extending flight time and operational efficiency across variable mission profiles.

🔋 AI & EV

🔋 EV Published
AI for Battery State of Charge (SoC) Estimation
This study proposes a RNN-CNN-based parallel hybrid model for accurate state of charge (SoC) estimation in Li-ion batteries used in electric vehicles. Trained on voltage, current, and temperature data, the model achieves high accuracy (RMSE 0.512%, MAE 0.354%, MaxE 1.98%, R² 99.94%) and outperforms the attention-based TCN (ATCN) model.
🔋 EV Published
AI for Estimating State of Health & Remaining Useful Life of EV Batteries
Systematic review of AI methods for SoH estimation and RUL prediction across diverse EV battery chemistries and operating conditions. Published in ICT Express, Elsevier, 2025.

Enhancing Smart Grid Resilience Through Privacy-Preserving and Decentralized Technologies
Integrating federated learning, blockchain, and AI enhances cybersecurity in smart grids by enabling strong intrusion detection, secure data sharing, and privacy protection across decentralized systems. These technologies also support secure energy trading, accurate load forecasting, and efficient resource scheduling, improving the resilience and operational efficiency of cyber-physical power systems.
